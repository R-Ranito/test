The importance of project management

Projects start to go wrong when people don't know their tasks and deadlines. Knowing how to manage projects involves discussing a detailed plan for the project—which includes specifying the project scope, roles, activities, and schedule. This allows a project team to work together more efficiently and effectively than if they just rolled up their sleeves and got down to business without having that much-needed clarity. The project management process involves anticipating these types of risks and identifying a plan B. This helps to overcome obstacles when they arise without losing steam during the course of the entire project.

Why is testing important?

Quality is directly linked to customer satisfaction. The more it meets expectations, the better the degree of customer satisfaction. In fact, no customer likes to receive software that always gives errors or does not work. Software quality also implies, even if indirectly, the number of users and customers. When something is really good, your customer can probably recommend it to other people. In addition, this customer will become loyal. Therefore, testing is super important for business success. Testing aims to ensure that all requirements are being met, that all functionality is being performed correctly, that more complex operations are being supported and that, obviously, there is no no problem in the application. Imagine that, after the delivery of the software, the customer points out an error in the system, that several things did not work as they should or that even an error message appeared. With proper software testing, you would be able to verify this incorrect situation even before the customer – which is very important.

Standardization lets you manage your project

Ensure information is standardized and, if your project has connections to other projects or processes, centralize everything in a single portal. Add categories, documents and links so your internal and external customers can find everything they need in one place. Centralize the exchange of information with teammates, customers and suppliers, so everyone can see everything, at once. Communicate via email and messages manually or automatically without leaving the platform.

Monitoring of procedure

However, it is important that there is standardization, monitoring of procedures, adequate information sharing, analysis and reporting. Therefore, software that serves as a project management system is undoubtedly essential for better results. It consists of a system or set of tools and techniques that basically organize, integrate and display project management results. Although the core is project management, the software that make up the SIGP can be divided to control specific areas of knowledge: risks, focus on quality or strategic management, for example. When it comes to the team, the project management system optimizes collaboration between members and improves their productivity, but goes beyond team management: GIS solutions standardize and manage the entire project lifecycle, from initiation, planning , monitoring and control until closure.
